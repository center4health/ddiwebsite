Transitions

Designing for Change

Whether it is travelling, moving, starting college or just replacing sensors, changes make it harder to manage glucose levels, supplies and social support. In the winter quarter of 2020, 19 UCSD students explored how to support Dexcom users in these critical moments. Several students wore a CGM themselves to better understand the needs of users. Based on this knowledge they interviewed people with diabetes, mapped their experience and developed new and innovative solutions for the uncovered issues in an 8 week sprint. Each solution was iteratively refined by feedback from participants with type 1 diabetes.

After several rounds of testing, the final feedback was overwhelmingly positive.

"This is dynamite!"

Margi, T1 Participant

"This would make [taking exams] so much easier."

Marlon, college student with T1D

"Would be very useful in the setting of a diabetes camp."

Gayle, RN, CDE, Camp Counselor

The final presentation to Dexcom was an overwhelming success. Each team pitched their solution, and presented an interactive prototype.
