Troubleshooting Technology

Designing to Empower the User

“With an extraordinary level of compassion and insight, the students were able to understand the service-related issues patients face and provide relevant, useful prototypes.”
Nolan Rey, Process Owner Global Technical Support - Dexcom

We developed solutions that empower people with Type-1 Diabetes to independently troubleshoot common issues with Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM). A 10 person delegation from Dexcom, including the Director of User Experience Design, the Director of Customer Experience and the Senior Product Manager visited the Design Lab to review five prototype solutions that were developed and tested by student groups. The feedback from Dexcom, including the discussions Dexcom had with each team, were overwhelmingly positive. Several of the prototype solutions will be considered in Dexcom’s upcoming product planning.

Five student teams, 21 students in total, worked for seven weeks using a human-centered design process to generate the design solutions. Dexcom provided access to their customer support reports, and mentored students throughout the seven weeks. In this rapid design sprint, teams interviewed people with Type 1 Diabetes as well as various experts from Dexcom to create and test new prototype solutions to empower patients. Each week, students learned how to design and develop effective healthcare technology by working on a real-world problem with an industry partner.
