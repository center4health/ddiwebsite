Participatory Design Online

“The design students developed some really exciting prototypes! They have come a long way and we’ve enjoying learning with them.”
Alex Diener, Director of UXD, Dexcom

Dexcom provided our teams with mentorship specific to their design challenge with value placed on learning, encouragement to freely design, and dynamic ways to incorporate feedback our students designed beyond their own expectations.

Dexcom’s team provided key insight, support, and guidance through the design challenge from product and customers experience managers to data science and UX design managers all contributing their experience and expertise.

Designing for People Living With Diabetes

Our design students began by listening and learning about their users; people living with diabetes, their parents and loved ones. The challenges of managing diabetes are complicated and multidimensional. It was important for our students to understand these challenges from multiple points of view. These lessons from interviews with people living with diabetes, family and healthcare providers filled in the gaps for their design approaches.

“Thank you all for the work you're doing. It's both the big things and the little things that make living with diabetes every day a little better.”
Chris, Participant

Interviews were conducted using video communications followed by need-finding to discover a user’s needs and opportunities for improvements with their continuous glucose monitors. Students used personas, empathy mapping and user journeys and weekly workshops with Dexcom stakeholders to extract key insights to help guide their design process.

Over several weeks, four teams built on insights from patient interviews and conducted weekly participatory design sessions to guide and define potential troubleshooting designs.

Experience & Insights

Students learned about diabetes management: the challenges and opportunities of using a continuous glucose monitor. They also heard from parents and their support networks including care teams from UCSD Health Systems and worked with a Dexcom interdisciplinary team. The design workshop was conducted by remote collaboration using Zoom, Figma and Miro. The applications allowed for rapid iteration and constant feedback.

Students learned from and tested their ideas with 25 participants varying in age (14 to 71), ethnicity, gender, and level of experience with continuous glucose monitoring. They also worked with healthcare providers: Endocrinologists, Physician Assistants, Nurse Practitioners and Pharmacists managing patients with diabetes via Telemedicine.

“DDI was an amazing learning experience! I’m so grateful to have worked with real industry professionals at Dexcom who provided wonderful mentorship and guidance that pushed me to grow immensely as a designer and researcher.”
Amanda, Design Student

Our design students and Dexcom stakeholders learned from our users who provided deep insight into the many challenges of managing diabetes. The technology of continuous glucose monitoring has given people with diabetes so much hope in their ability but has also shown new opportunities for improvement, meaningful data and ways to receive support both online and offline.

Our students experienced an opportunity to move the needle forward in our future of health. Together with the support of the diabetes community, the future is very bright.
