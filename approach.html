Our Approach

Envision and facilitate the development of better technology for people with diabetes

Our mission is to make diabetes as invisible as possible by taking a people-centered approach to diabetes technology. We work with leading manufacturers, members of the diabetes community, patient advocates, researchers, clinicians, government and industry. Together we aim to push the boundaries of technology with a strong focus on empathy and human needs.

Our work is guided by the following principles:

  1. Focus on people, from people with diabetes, their parents, lead users, community volunteers and patient advocates to researchers, clinicians, government and industry partners
  2. Treat diabetes care as a complex socio-technical system, not a collection of isolated tools and tasks
  3. Foster human-technology teamwork by designing technologies that work with, rather than direct people.
  4. Design for continuous ground-up innovation, empowering people with diabetes to live their lifes as they want.

The Design Lab is located within the California Institute of Telecommunication and Information Technology, which gives us freedom to move seamlessly across all the departments, schools, and divisions of the university. Our advisory board includes people from Management, Engineering, Visual Arts, Theater, and the Social Sciences.
